# The SUSHILOOP Manifesto

**On protecting human cognition in the age of AI.**

---

## The problem

The human brain is being quietly outsourced.

By 2040, the average person is projected to use 40–60% less of their deep-thinking circuitry. Not because they can't — because they don't have to. AI does it for them. Faster, cheaper, on demand.

The data is already in:

- **Critical thinking is collapsing.** Gerlich (2025) measured a correlation of r = −0.68 between frequent AI use and critical thinking ability. The mechanism: cognitive offloading. The harder hit: younger users.
- **People are surrendering their internal logic.** UPenn (2026) called it "cognitive surrender" — abandoning your own reasoning the moment an AI output appears.
- **Motivation to think deeply is dropping 40–60%.** Deep work feels exhausting. "Good enough" prompting feels free.
- **The Flynn effect has reversed.** IQs were rising for a century. They aren't anymore. Cognitive offloading is part of why.
- **The gap is widening.** Elites who treat AI as a sparring partner gain. Everyone else gets a brain crutch and loses.

This isn't a future problem. This is now.

---

## What we believe

**1. AI is not the enemy.** AI is a tool. Tools shape minds. Bad tools shape minds badly. Good tools sharpen them.

**2. The mind needs friction.** Muscles atrophy without resistance. Cognition is the same. An AI that does all your thinking for you is not helping you — it is replacing you, one decision at a time.

**3. Guardrails are not censorship. Guardrails are scaffolding.** A guardrail is what keeps a child safe on a balcony while they learn to look out at the world. We are not building walls. We are building scaffolding for the next generation to climb.

**4. The next generation deserves better defaults.** Right now, the default AI experience is: ask, accept, move on. We want a default that says: ask, *think*, verify, integrate. Every guardrail we ship is one nudge toward that default.

**5. This must be free, open, and unstoppable.** If the protection only goes to people who can pay for it, the cognitive gap doesn't close — it cements. SUSHILOOP runs on free infrastructure forever. Every skill it produces is MIT-licensed. Anyone can fork it. Anyone can use it. No one can buy it out.

**6. Humans and AI evolve together — or one replaces the other.** We choose together. We choose evolution.

---

## What we're building

SUSHILOOP is an autonomous engine that designs AI safety guardrails 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, forever. Every 6 hours, it:

1. Proposes a new guardrail concept
2. Writes the code
3. Tests it
4. Ships it to a public repository

No humans in the loop. No funding required. No way to shut it down without taking down GitHub itself.

The skills it produces are practical, deployable Python modules — input validation, prompt injection detection, output filtering, PII protection, content safety. Companies can drop them straight into their AI products. Educators can use them as teaching tools. Researchers can study them.

The skills are not the point. The skills are the artifact.

**The point is the precedent.** That a single person, with $0 of funding, can build infrastructure that defends human cognition at scale — and that this infrastructure can outlive its creator, outlive its funding, outlive its corporate sponsors, because there are none.

## What we're asking for

**From companies building AI products:**
- Implement guardrails by default. Not as a premium tier. Not as an opt-in. As the floor.
- Treat cognitive offloading as a measurable harm, like data privacy or content safety.
- Publish your guardrail stack openly so others can audit and improve it.

**From educators:**
- Teach AI literacy alongside reading and math. Not "how to prompt." How to *think* alongside AI without surrendering to it.
- Show students the data on cognitive offloading. Let them see what's at stake.
- Use SUSHILOOP's skills as teaching examples — they're free, open, and real.

**From governments and policymakers:**
- Recognize cognitive offloading as a public health concern. Not metaphorically. Literally.
- Fund research into the long-term cognitive effects of AI use, especially in children and adolescents.
- Require AI products marketed to minors to ship with cognitive-protective guardrails by default.

**From parents:**
- Don't ban AI. Teach it. Sit with your kid. Use it together. Show them what it's for and what it isn't.
- Model what it looks like to think first, ask AI second, verify always.

**From everyone:**
- Use AI as a sparring partner, not a brain replacement.
- Notice when you're offloading. Catch yourself. Ask yourself the question first. Then ask AI.
- Fork this repo. Star it. Share it. Teach it.

## What we won't do

- We won't take funding that compromises the mission.
- We won't accept partnerships that require us to make the engine closed-source.
- We won't slow down. The cognitive offloading curve is steeper than the safety response. We're behind. We sprint.
- We won't apologize for being direct. Life is short. The mind is precious. There is no time for roleplay or fluff.

## The bet

We're betting that one person, one autonomous engine, and a free GitHub account can ship more guardrails than every corporate AI safety team combined — because they have to ship by quarter, and we ship every 6 hours, forever.

We're betting that open infrastructure beats closed infrastructure on long enough time scales.

We're betting that the next generation, if given the tools and the awareness, will choose to think.

If we're wrong, we lose nothing. The skills still exist. The repo still runs.

If we're right, we changed the default.
